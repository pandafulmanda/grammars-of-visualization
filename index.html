Grammar

noun : \ˈgra-mər\

  1. the whole system and structure of a language or of languages in general, usually taken as consisting of syntax and morphology (including inflections) and sometimes also phonology and semantics.
  2. the principles or rules of an art, science, or technique; also : a set of such principles or rules

A grammar helps us express.

Grammar makes language expressive. A language consisting of words and no grammar expresses only as many ideas as there are words. By specifying how words are combined in statements, a grammar expands a language's scope.

Leland Wilkinson

A grammar helps us think.

A good grammar will allow us to gain insight into the composition of complicated graphics, and reveal unexpected connections between seemingly different graphics.

Hadley Wickham

A grammar helps us communicate.

It helps us describe graphics to each other and to tools that make the graphics.
