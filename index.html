Grammar

noun : \ˈgra-mər\

  1. the whole system and structure of a language or of languages in general, usually taken as consisting of syntax and morphology (including inflections) and sometimes also phonology and semantics.
  2. the principles or rules of an art, science, or technique; also : a set of such principles or rules

A grammar helps us express.

Grammar makes language expressive. A language consisting of words and no grammar expresses only as many ideas as there are words. By specifying how words are combined in statements, a grammar expands a language's scope.

Leland Wilkinson

A grammar helps us think.

A good grammar will allow us to gain insight into the composition of complicated graphics, and reveal unexpected connections between seemingly different graphics.

Hadley Wickham

A grammar helps us communicate.

It helps us describe graphics to each other and to tools that make the graphics. A grammar helps us share graphics.

The Grammar of Graphics

By Leland Wilkinson, 1999

If we endeavor to develop a charting instead of a graphing program, we will accomplish two things. First, we inevitably will offer fewer charts than people want. Second, our package will have no deep structure. Our computer program will be unnecessarily complex, because we will fail to reuse objects or routines that function similarly in different charts. And we will have no way to add new charts to our system without generating complex new code. Elegant design requires us to think about a theory of graphics, not charts.

Polaris

A System for Query, Analysis, and Visualization of Multidimensional Relational Databases

By Chris Stolte, Diane Tang, and Pat Hanrahan, 2001

aimed to enable rapid exploratory analysis with expressive visuals for data in relational databases

Specification

  • Table configurations across panes
    • Mapping of data sources to layers
    • Rows, columns, and layers
    • Selection and partitioning of records
    • Grouping of data and computation
  • Type of graphic inside panes
  • Details of visual encodings
    • Mapping of data fields to retinal properties

Interactions for exploration

  • Deriving additional fields
  • Sorting and filtering
  • Brushing and tooltips
  • Undo and redo
  • Zooming
